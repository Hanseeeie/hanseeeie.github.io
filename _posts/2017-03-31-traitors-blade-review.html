About

Set in a fantasy world, this story focuses on a trio of 'Greatcoats'. So named because of their trademarked coats - veritable treasure troves of weapons, defences and supplies which can keep them alive for weeks whist travelling.

They were re-formed by the late King in order to study and uphold the King's laws throughout the land - including within the duchies. The dukes and duchesses, used to having their own laws enforced within their respective duchies, banded together to 'remove' the King and disband the Greatcoats.

The world in which we join the story is one where the Greatcoats are seen as both traitors to the duchies and traitors to the King.

Liked

Characters! The characters were very well written - each with their own quirks and personalities.

Falcio val Mond

As the story follows Falcio val Mond and his journey, it's easy to like his character. He takes charge in a way that feels very dependable, and his two Greatcoat companions Kest and Brasti react to him this way. Falcio has a fierce determination to do what's right, a fierce loyalty to the King and his friends and a fierce love for his wife - everything he does, he does with his heart. He's the true embodiment of the King's Heart (which I think would also have been a lovely name for the book!).

Kest

There's not a lot I can say about Kest without ruining the story! Kest has this calm, critical and calculating view of the world. I feel that he sees things in a black or white way - there's no grey area, and only the influence of Falcio and Brasti stop him acting on his own beliefs.

Brasti

Brasti is the comic relief in the novel - in a good way! When the tension becomes unbearable (and it will!) you can count on Brasti to make some remark which will have you laughing out loud.

Valiana

I found myself instantly drawn to Valiana. I don't know if it's because I didn't trust her or because of the mystery surrounding her. Her character produced a love/hate reaction from me with each passing chapter!

The Tailor

Completely mad, completely brilliant and completely frustrating! I hope there's more of her to come.

The Fey Horse

The idea of a fabled horse that could understand humans was so exciting to me and I love the poor creature that is Monster.

Overall

de Castell's novel hooked me from the get-go with mystery and the promise of a new fantasy world.

It made me laugh, it made me cry, it made me angry, and it made me so incredibly frustrated - but in a good way! It had so many twists and turns I couldn't even begin to guess what would happen, and I was glued to the book until I found out.

Favourite quote

'Gods, man, don't you start now,' I said softly. 'We're going to get a terrible reputation if we just keep travelling across the countryside crying all the time.' - p. 361

If you are a fan of adult fantasy, like a bit of mystery, love swordplay and want to experience a great new world filled with diverse characters, then this novel is definitely for you.

Rating 5/5

As I can't wait for the next novel in the series to come out, I'm currently finding out all I can to join the Greatcoats!
